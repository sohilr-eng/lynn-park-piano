Biography

Meet Lynn Park

Education & Training

Lynn Park's passion for music has been a constant thread throughout her life — shaping how she thinks, feels, and creates. That passion led her to pursue formal study at Rutgers University, where she earned a Bachelor of Arts in Music Composition.

Her training gave her a deep understanding of how music is constructed at every level — from the interplay of harmony and rhythm, to the architecture of longer musical forms. It was this compositional lens that set the foundation for everything she creates and teaches today.

"Understanding how music is built — how a phrase breathes, how harmony creates tension, how rhythm drives emotion — is what separates a player from a true musician. That is what I want to give my students."

Compositional Work

As a composer, Lynn writes music for solo piano that is deeply personal and immediately felt. Her sound world is nostalgic, melancholy, and calming — characterised by lyrical expressiveness, gentle rhythmic vitality, and a seamless blend of classical structure with contemporary sensibility.

Her compositions invite the listener into a reflective space — music that feels like a memory, or a quiet moment held still. Notable original works include "Jellyfish," "HIRATH," and "Mutual Understanding" — each a distinct meditation on emotion and sound.

Teaching Philosophy

Lynn established her piano teaching practice in 2025 with a clear and personal mission: to help students truly understand and love music — not just see it as a chore or a task to check off.

Her approach is rooted in nurturing genuine musical appreciation. She believes that when a student understands why music works — why a melody pulls at the heart, why a chord creates suspense — practice stops feeling like obligation and starts feeling like discovery. Her background as a composer means she brings this insight into every lesson, at every level.

Lynn adapts her teaching style to the individual. For young children, lessons are playful and imaginative — building a joyful relationship with the instrument from day one. For older students and adults, she introduces compositional thinking early so students can hear the music they play in a richer, more meaningful way.

Beyond the Studio

When she is not composing or teaching, Lynn enjoys spending time in nature, staying active, and listening to music across a wide range of styles. She is a regular concert-goer, attending live performances — mostly in New York City — and finds that experiencing music as a listener continuously informs her as a musician and educator.

Notable Compositions

A selection of Lynn's original piano compositions — each a reflection of her distinctive musical voice.

Original Composition

Jellyfish

A floating, luminous piece that evokes the weightless drift of its namesake — gentle waves of melody over a calm, unhurried harmonic bed.

Original Composition

HIRATH

Inspired by the Welsh concept of longing for a home you can't return to, this piece is deeply melancholic — a meditation on memory and belonging.

Original Composition

Mutual Understanding

A quietly conversational work — two melodic voices finding their way toward one another, reflecting the beauty of connection through music.
